Most small businesses don’t struggle because they lack effort.

They struggle because everyday workflows are still too manual.

Especially accounting.

Even today, many business owners are:

switching between spreadsheets and apps
manually entering expenses
creating invoices repeatedly
tracking income in disconnected systems

The problem isn’t a lack of features.

The problem is complexity.

Most accounting tools were designed like traditional enterprise software:
dashboards, menus, forms, reports everywhere.

But small businesses and freelancers often need something simpler.

That’s one of the reasons I started building FintraBooks.

The idea behind FintraBooks is straightforward:

Instead of filling forms and navigating complex menus, users can simply type naturally:

“Spent ₹500 on fuel”
“Create invoice ₹10,000”
“Show today’s report”

And the system handles the organization automatically.

The goal isn’t to replace accountants.

It’s to reduce repetitive manual work and make everyday business operations faster and less stressful.

I believe the future of business software will become more conversational:
less clicking, less data entry, more natural workflows.

We already chat all day using messaging apps.

Why shouldn’t business management work the same way?

Still early in the journey, but building FintraBooks has made one thing very clear:

Small businesses don’t necessarily want more features.

They want simplicity.
